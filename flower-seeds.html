Annuals

Life Cycle: Annual plants complete their entire life cycle—from germination to seed production to death—within a single growing season, which typically lasts one year.

Growth Period: They sprout, grow, bloom, produce seeds, and die all in one year.

Replanting: Annuals need to be replanted every year if you want them to grow again.

Examples: Common examples of annuals include marigolds, petunias, zinnias, and most vegetable crops like tomatoes, beans, and lettuce.

Perinnials

Life Cycle: Perennial plants live for more than two years. They go through repeated cycles of growth and blooming year after year.

Growth Period: After the initial planting, perennials will grow and bloom each season and then die back to the ground level during the off-season (often winter), regrowing from their root systems in the following season.

Replanting: Perennials do not need to be replanted every year. Once established, they can continue to grow and bloom for several years.

Examples: Common examples of perennials include peonies, hostas, daylilies, and many types of shrubs and trees.
